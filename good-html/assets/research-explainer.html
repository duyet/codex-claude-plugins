The Mechanics of Zero-Knowledge Proofs

An interactive deep-dive into cryptographic privacy.

The Prover and the Verifier

In a zero-knowledge proof, one party (the prover) can prove to another (the verifier) that they know a value, without conveying any information apart from the fact that they know the value.

Underlying Hard Problems

Most ZK-SNARKs rely on the hardness of discrete logarithms or similar group-theoretic properties.
